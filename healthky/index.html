Overview

The Map

This map is a modest attempt to provide a cross-sectional overview of the prevalence and expenditure per capita of 21 common chronic conditions across the counties of Kentucky. Key features include an Analysis tab panel that presents additional information on the expenditure per capita per county per chronic conditions as well as the the Social Vulnerability Index 2018 (SVI) for each respective county. A bar graph depicting the selected chronic condition (dropdown) between 2010-2017 for the county selected (Tooltip).

Chronic Conditions

Originally intended to visualize only heart-related chronic conditions, the project has evolved to favor a wider visualization spectrum to instill and stimulate honest appreciation and understanding of the relationship between health and the socioeconomic conditions unique to each counties of Kentucky. Personally, in the process of making this map, it is difficult to escape lingering thoughts “What are the probability of becoming part of the statistics?" In Kentucky and elsewhere in our nation, the health status is equally bleak.

Social Vulnerability Index

Before addressing “The Social Vulnerability Index”, it is helpful to glance at the Social Determinants of Health (SDOH). Conceived by the CDC, it is a relatively new concept stating that "conditions in the places where people live, learn, work, and play affect a range of health risks and outcomes". Indeed, there is growing evidence to suggest that social determinants such as unstable housing, low income, unsafe neighborhoods, or substandard education will have a negative impact on health. SODH is important to improve population health and foster health equity.

As our country is grappling with runaway healthcare costs, the SODH may present a rare opportunity for a substantial paradigm shift from traditional treatment-first to prevention-first philosophy. It may present invaluable opportunity for policymakers and healthcare professionals to rethink the different ways and means to distribute valuable health resources. For the citizenry, understanding SODH can nurture the growth of an inclusive grassroots activism toward a cleaner, healthier and equitable living conditions.

The Social Vulnerability Index can be described as a scorecard to measure the level of community's resilience when confronted by external stresses on human health including stresses from natural and human-caused disasters or disease outbreaks. Lowering social vulnerability can decrease both human suffering and economic loss.

In this map we included 4 themes described in the SVI: 1) Socioeconomic; 2) Household Composition & Disability; 3) Minority Status & Language; 4) Housing Type & Transportation. A summary ranking therme was also included in the analysis. SVI Documentation

Applications

In the Analysis tab, a dashboard-inspired panel is used to display key indicators: 1) prevalence (County, Kentucky Average, and US Average); 2) SVI themes; and 3) expenditure per capita. The tooltip on the map also provides indicators. However, it is limited in the display of those related to prevalence. It is intended that the map when used in combination with the Analysis panel can provide a useful high-level view of the extent of the specific chronic conditions by counties and its relationships to key SVI theme indicators and expense per capita. For instance, several inferences can be derived on the characteristics of health expenses in, say, less affluent counties of Eastern Kentucky versus a more urbanized counties like Jefferson and Fayette. Furthermore, inferences can be made on the level of prevalence of specific types of chronic conditions and its comparative costs based on the level of income and urbanization.

In sum, this analysis should provide useful overview for medical researchers and policymakers in the area of grant applications with regards to formulating programs to target the right resources to address the chronic conditions that exists in the counties.

Key Improvements

Improvements will significantly depens on continuing development and acquisition of technical and design skills and experience. Deployment of the right tools for the intended purpose of the project is critical.

However, many important lessons learned:

  • Data: SQL and database related skills are primary skills when work involved large datasets. Continued understanding of the use of CSV format data in geospatial analysis could lessen the burden of data wrangling.
  • Work Breakdown Structure: Tasks breakdown and resource estimate with a strong project management approach is critical for planning as well as renumeration purposes.

    • This map was conceived from the perspective of a designer not a software developer. The best UI to visualize the data would be to deploy the swipe capability between two maps capability. One map should show the vulnerability index and the other the chronic conditions and/or expenditure per capita per county.
