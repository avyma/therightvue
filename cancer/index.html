Cancer Death Rates Across US Counties, 2009-2013

Authored by Vu Nguyen

The Cancer Death Rates by County 2009-2013 map was built under contract with specific requirements by The National Cancer Institute. With this map, it is hope that it will raise awareness of the extent of cancer deaths in the United States. The map is a web interactive choropleth map type using a 7 class quantile classification method. It was constructed completely using CARTO map. The purple color is symbolic of the color of cancer. The map has been projected to an equal area CRS - EPSG:2163 (US National Atlas Equal Area). The map requirements are listed below:

  • Illustrated the distribution of cancer rates averaged over a 5-year period by US county.
  • Featured a window pop-up allowing the user to derive the name of the county, the state name, the death rate, and trend upon clicking a county.
  • Included a user-friendly legend
  • Overlaid state boundar
  • Map and layered metadata
  • Link to full-screen version provided

Data source and method

The data describe cancer rates for all cancer sites by mortality, for all races, sexes, and ages, averaged over the years of 2009 - 2013. Rates are the number of deaths per 100,000 people.
